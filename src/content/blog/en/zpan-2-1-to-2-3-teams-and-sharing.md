The first three releases after the rewrite answered a practical question: how does a personal file manager become a platform people can safely use together?

## Identity and onboarding in 2.1

ZPan 2.1 added a complete authentication and onboarding flow, including OAuth, invitations, and email delivery. A fresh deployment could guide its first administrator through setup, then control how additional users joined.

## Teams and spaces in 2.2

Version 2.2 introduced teams, workspaces, and member invitations. Files no longer had to live in one undifferentiated personal area. People could move between personal and shared contexts while administrators managed membership centrally.

## Sharing that works outside the app in 2.3

Version 2.3 completed the collaboration loop with public landing pages, direct links, passwords, expiration, download limits, folder browsing, and save-to-drive. Notifications keep recipients informed without turning every transfer into a manual conversation.

Together, these releases made ZPan useful on both sides of a link: structured for the people managing files, and straightforward for the people receiving them.
